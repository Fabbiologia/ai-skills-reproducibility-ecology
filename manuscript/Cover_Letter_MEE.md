**Re: Submission of an original Research Article, “Reusable instructions make AI ecological analyses reproducible when they remove analytical choices.”**

Dear Editors,

We submit a controlled study of when reusable AI analysis instructions improve ecological output reproducibility. Rather than asking whether longer prompts help, the study isolates contracts, stochastic controls, and validation guardrails, and separates repeated-run agreement from validity against an independent reference.

Experiment 1 crossed four tasks, five cumulative instruction levels, Python and R implementations, and ten runs per cell (400 runs). A secondary 48-report experiment tested whether the mechanism extends to multi-analysis deliverables. The clearest results are conditional: fixed stochastic controls removed classifier variation in Python and aligned both implementations with their own references; an explicit survey-unit contract corrected reef-fish biomass outputs that were perfectly repeatable but invalid; generic extra steps did not help.

The contribution is both empirical and infrastructural. We distinguish what the experiments directly support from what a repository should additionally require, and provide a machine-readable Skill Evidence Manifest, validator, worked skill entry, and archive audit. Candidate skills face separate self-consistency, reference-validity, and method-coherence gates.

The study fits *Methods in Ecology and Evolution* because it converts a rapidly spreading practice into explicit estimands, testable failure modes, and reusable validation infrastructure. It also reports important boundaries: the study was not preregistered, each main mechanism is supported by one task, historical model metadata are incomplete, the reef-fish data are restricted, and report grading is only partly auditable.

We confirm that this manuscript is original, has not been published before, and is not under consideration elsewhere, that all authors have approved the submission, and that we have no competing interests. Public code, open data, structured outputs, and documents will be archived with a DOI; restricted data are available on request.
